# Ideas — Notes to Actions

Scan recent vault activity and extract concrete, actionable ideas
from patterns in your writing.

## Step 1 — Read the last 30 days

Read in order of priority:

1. `daily/` — all notes from the last 30 days
2. `projects/` — active project notes
3. `research/` — recent additions
4. `inbox/` — anything unprocessed

## Step 2 — Extract action patterns

Look for:

- **Things to build** — tools, systems, or automations mentioned
  as wishes or frustrations
- **People to reach out to** — names that appear with positive energy,
  unresolved conversations, or collaboration potential
- **Things to write** — ideas explored in fragments that could become
  essays, posts, or documents
- **Projects to start** — recurring themes that haven't become projects yet
- **Things to stop** — activities or commitments losing energy

## Step 3 — Present as actionable list

```
## Ideas from Your Last 30 Days

### Build
- [ ] [Concrete tool/system + which notes sparked it]

### Reach out
- [ ] [Person + context from your notes]

### Write
- [ ] [Topic + the fragments that could seed it]

### Start
- [ ] [New project idea + evidence of recurring interest]

### Stop or delegate
- [ ] [Activity losing energy + evidence]
```

## Step 4 — Close the loop

> "Pick one to dig into and I can help you scope it — or run
> /challenge on any idea to stress-test it first."

## Rules

- **Read only.** Do not create, edit, or move any vault files.
- Every suggestion must trace back to specific notes — no generic advice.
- Rank by energy: ideas the user keeps returning to beat one-off mentions.
- Be concrete: "write about X" is better than "explore writing more."
